# Basic Moss Wall Installation Guide

A step-by-step guide to creating your own live moss wall at home.

## Overview

Building a moss wall is a rewarding DIY project that brings natural beauty indoors. This guide covers the basics of creating a simple, maintainable moss wall installation.

## Materials Needed

### Frame and Backing
- **Frame:** Wood shadow box, picture frame, or custom-built frame
- **Backing board:** Plywood, cork board, or foam board
- **Waterproof barrier:** Plastic sheeting or pond liner

### Substrate (choose one)
- Sphagnum moss (recommended for beginners)
- Sheet moss base layer
- Cork bark pieces
- Mesh grid system

### Moss
- Live sheet moss (Hypnum)
- Mood moss (Dicranum) for texture
- Mixed moss varieties for visual interest

### Tools and Supplies
- Staple gun or hot glue gun
- Spray bottle
- Wire or fishing line (optional, for securing)
- Mounting hardware

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare the Frame

1. Select a frame appropriate for your space
2. Line the back with waterproof plastic sheeting
3. Secure the backing board to the frame
4. Ensure adequate depth (at least 2 inches recommended)

### Step 2: Create the Substrate Layer

**Sphagnum moss method (recommended):**
1. Soak sphagnum moss in water for 15 minutes
2. Squeeze out excess water (should be damp, not dripping)
3. Spread evenly across the backing, 1-2 inches thick
4. Secure with staples or mesh overlay

**Cork bark method:**
1. Arrange cork pieces on backing
2. Secure with waterproof adhesive
3. Fill gaps with sphagnum moss

### Step 3: Attach the Live Moss

1. Mist the substrate thoroughly
2. Place moss pieces on the substrate, pressing gently
3. Start from the bottom and work upward
4. Overlap edges slightly for seamless coverage
5. Mix textures for visual interest (optional)

### Step 4: Secure the Moss

Choose one method:
- **Natural adhesion:** Press firmly and mist; moss will root over time
- **Pins or staples:** Use sparingly to hold moss in place
- **Mesh overlay:** Stretch garden mesh over moss temporarily

### Step 5: Initial Care

1. Mist thoroughly after installation
2. Place in a shaded area for 2-3 weeks
3. Mist daily during establishment period
4. Avoid direct sunlight completely at first

## Mounting Options

### Wall-Mounted
- Use D-rings and picture wire
- Ensure frame can handle weight when wet
- Consider wall material for appropriate anchors

### Freestanding
- Add easel back to frame
- Ensure stability when watering
- Good for tabletop displays

### Vertical Garden System
- Use modular panels
- Allows for easier maintenance
- Scalable for larger installations

## Maintenance After Installation

**First 2-3 weeks:**
- Mist daily
- Keep away from direct light
- Check for any loose pieces

**Ongoing care:**
- Mist 2-3 times per week (adjust based on humidity)
- Trim any brown edges with scissors
- Remove any debris or fallen pieces

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too much direct sunlight** — Will scorch the moss
2. **Over-watering** — Leads to mold and rot
3. **Under-watering** — Causes browning and dormancy
4. **Poor drainage** — Trapped water causes problems
5. **Rushing establishment** — Give moss time to adapt
